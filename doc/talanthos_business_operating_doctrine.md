# Talanthos Business Operating Doctrine

Version: 1.0
Purpose: master document for strategy, funnels, products, AI context, development, and future decisions.

This document is the primary source of truth for Talanthos. Any discussion, development, prompt, funnel, report, landing page, ad creative, or product decision must respect the logic defined here.

---

## 1. Strategic summary

Talanthos must not be treated as a financial app, a budgeting tool, or a Christian money-coaching brand.

Talanthos is a system of personalized biblical interpretation that helps a person see what their relationship with money reveals about their soul, and what God is inviting them to repair, refine, or release.

Primary positioning:

> The Bible says more about money than almost any other subject, because how a person holds money reveals how they hold everything. Talanthos helps people see what their money is telling them about their heart.

What we actually sell:

- clarity
- spiritual self-recognition
- a hard truth delivered with compassion
- direction rooted in Scripture
- the sense that "this was written for me"
- a turning point, not just information

What we do NOT sell:

- financial advice that requires a license
- prosperity gospel or wealth promises
- budgeting techniques or money mechanics
- guilt, fear, or manipulation
- a generic PDF
- a quick fix

Central principle:

> The user is not buying a document. They are buying a moment of self-recognition that calls them toward change.

## 2. Brand philosophy

Talanthos must feel:

- reverent, but not religious-kitsch
- serious, but not heavy
- spiritual, but grounded in reality
- personal, but not invasive
- confronting, but never arrogant
- premium, but accessible
- editorial, like a magazine you keep, not a funnel you forget

The brand must avoid the zones of "prosperity preacher," "money coach," "televangelist," "Christian Instagram quote account," and "generic AI content."

The ideal voice is a wise pastoral mentor who loves the reader enough to tell them the hard truth.

Tone:

- calm
- certain
- compassionate
- direct
- literary
- mature
- unflinching but hopeful

Example of good direction:

> Your forward instinct is not wrong, but it is untethered. You do not lack vision. You lack the patience to let one seed bear fruit before planting the next.

Example of wrong direction:

> Discover the secret God wants you to know to unlock financial breakthrough and abundance in 30 days.

## 3. The Talanthos promise

The promise must be framed as interpretation and invitation, never as guaranteed outcome.

Correct formula:

> Based on your answers, Talanthos names the way God has wired you to relate to money, the blind spot most likely to cost you, and the next step Scripture sets before you.

Wrong formula:

> Talanthos tells you exactly how to get out of debt and build wealth God's way.

Safety rule:

We never use language that promises financial returns, guarantees wealth, or replaces professional advice.

We may use:

- may reveal
- may suggest
- often shows up as
- can be a sign of
- in the language of Scripture
- in the posture of the heart
- the invitation here is

We avoid:

- guaranteed
- proven to make you money
- you will become wealthy
- the secret to riches
- financial breakthrough

Every money-topic section in the report carries a clear framing: this is spiritual guidance, not professional financial, legal, or tax advice.

- self-knowledge assessments (personality types, archetypes)
- biblical / Christian spirituality
- stewardship and faith-and-finance teaching
- emotional and identity work
- editorial Christian publishing (Plough, Comment, The Rabbit Room)

Perceived direct competition:

- hard truth with compassion, not flattery or fear

Recommended positioning sentence:

> Talanthos is a biblical money type assessment that helps Christians see what their money reveals about their soul, and the path Scripture sets before them.

## 5. Primary avatar

Initial Talanthos avatar:

- Christian adults, roughly 30 to 55
- US and UK primary markets
- active on Pinterest, readers of faith content
- interested in self-knowledge, spiritual growth, stewardship, identity
- skeptical of prosperity gospel and televangelist culture
- have a quiet unease about money that budgeting has not resolved
- want depth, not another spreadsheet

Frequent emotional states:

- "Why do I keep repeating the same pattern with money?"
- "I feel anxious about money even when things are fine."
- "I want my finances to honor God but I do not know how."
- "I give too much / hold too tightly / chase too fast."
- "There has to be more to this than budgeting."

Desires:

- to be seen accurately
- to receive a personal, specific reading
- to find spiritual meaning in a practical struggle
- to be told the truth, even if it is hard
- to have a clear next step

Objections:

- "Is this generic?"
- "Is this a scam like the others?"
- "Is it worth nineteen dollars?"
- "Will I get something real or just AI text?"
- "Is it too 'churchy' or too 'woo'?"
- "Is it actually personalized?"

Strategic response to objections:

- show a genuine personalized preview on the result page
- use specific, literary language, not vague affirmations
- never overpromise
- transparent pricing, clear refund policy
- show exactly what the report contains
- use continuation CTAs, never aggressive selling

## 6. Core funnel principle

The Talanthos funnel must follow this architecture:

Ad or organic traffic
→ Landing page
→ Quiz (questions including context and free text)
→ Calculating / preparation screen
→ Intro reading (Scripture, heart preparation)
→ Result page (type reveal + AI-personalized "hard truth" preview, partly locked)
→ Email capture
→ Paywall ($19 personalized PDF report)
→ Stripe checkout
→ PDF generation (on purchase) + email delivery
→ Thank-you page (pay-it-forward offer)
→ Email nurture sequence for non-buyers

Objective of the funnel:

Turn curiosity into self-recognition, and self-recognition into a decision to go deeper.

Rule:

> The reader must feel they have already received a true and personal word, but that the fullness of it is still ahead.

## 7. Quiz rules

The quiz is not a survey. It is a guided act of self-recognition that also gathers the context the AI needs to write a genuinely personal reading.

General rules:

- mobile-first
- one question per screen
- visible progress
- no long text between questions
- contemplative pacing, not gamified hype
- event tracking on each step

Beyond the scoring questions (which map to the 4 types), the quiz must capture the context that powers personalization:

- age range
- marital status
- whether they have children
- current financial situation
- biggest financial regret (free text)
- how they feel about money today (free text)

The two free-text answers are critical. They are what allow the AI reading to feel written for this specific person, not generated for a category.

Recommended events:

- QuizStarted
- QuestionCompleted (per question)
- EmailStepViewed
- EmailSubmitted
- ResultViewed
- PaywallViewed
- CheckoutStarted
- PurchaseCompleted

Meta does not need many pageviews. It needs clear, consistent signals, with Purchase as the optimization event from day one.

---

## 8. The result page

The result page must do two things at once:

1. Deliver enough genuine, personalized value that the reader believes the system truly read them.
2. Leave enough incompleteness that the reader wants the full report.

Recommended structure:

1. Type reveal (monogram, name, biblical figure)
2. Score bars across the four dimensions
3. A short AI-personalized "hard truth" passage (the hook of the whole product)
4. Visible: strengths preview, key verse, one next step
5. Locked: the deeper blind spots, the full plan, the strategies
6. CTA toward the paywall

Rule:

The free reading must not feel like "enough." It must feel like the first true sentence of a longer word the reader now needs to hear in full.

## 9. Paywall psychology

The paywall must not feel like a separate sales page. It must feel like the natural continuation of the reading.

We do not sell:

- a PDF
- a document
- a report

We sell:

- the full personalized reading
- clarity
- the hard truth in full
- the path forward

Recommended CTAs:

- Receive your full reading
- Unlock the complete report
- See the rest of what your answers reveal
- Continue the reading

Weaker CTAs:

- Buy now
- Order the report
- Pay for the PDF

Recommended paywall structure:

1. Personalized confirmation (their type)
2. What they have discovered so far
3. What is still hidden
4. What the full report contains (the 8 promised sections)
5. Price and anchor ($19, anchored against $47)
6. Continuation CTA
7. Reassurance: instant delivery, secure checkout, no subscription, 30-day money-back guarantee
8. A short, honest reassurance of quality

Critical integrity rule:

The paywall promises exactly what the PDF delivers. Every value bullet on the paywall maps to a real section in the report. We never promise what we do not deliver. This is the line that separates Talanthos from Prayers Academy.

## 10. Pricing framework

Price must be tested, not assumed.

Phase 1 product:

- $19 one-time for the personalized PDF report
- anchored against a $47 reference (the sum of the eight components priced separately)

Rule:

We test both the price and the framing of the price.

Weak framing:

> 30-page PDF report, $19

Better framing:

> Your complete personalized reading, $19, yours forever, no subscription

Anchoring:

> The eight parts of this report, priced separately, total $47. Today, as one reading, $19.

Caution:

No false urgency, no fake countdowns, no manipulative scarcity. We may use honest framings: launch pricing, introductory price, founding-reader price. Nothing dishonest.

## 11. Product ecosystem

Talanthos must be built as an ecosystem of biblical interpretation, fazed strictly. Nothing in a later phase launches before the earlier phase has proven product-market fit.

Phase 1 (now):
1. The Quiz + AI Personalized Reading (free lead magnet)
2. The Full Report PDF ($19)

Phase 2 (after PMF, roughly 3 to 6 months, only if CAC < $25 and AOV > $30):
3. The Journal ($29 print / $9 digital): 90-day journaling prompts for your type

Phase 3 (after 200+ paying customers):
5. The Companion ($9/month subscription): weekly devotional plus monthly type-specific reading, all text or AI-audio, zero live

Each product must have:

- a clear hook
- an input or entry flow
- a personalized or type-specific output
- its own tracking
- contextual cross-sell logic

## 12. The 4 Biblical Money Types

Talanthos maps every reader onto one of four archetypes drawn from Scripture. Each has a biblical figure, a tagline, a Roman monogram, a cover verse, and a line-art symbol.

Type I, The Visionary, figure Solomon, "The Wisdom-Wealth Builder," verse 1 Kings 3:13, symbol a crown.
The reader who sees future possibility, moves toward opportunity, multiplies. Risk: speed outrunning counsel, vision without governance, building beyond what wisdom can hold.

Type II, The Guardian, figure Joseph, "The Steward-Protector," verse Genesis 41:49, symbol a grain sheaf.
The reader who protects, preserves, prepares for lean seasons. Risk: clinging to security, scarcity disguised as wisdom, holding too tightly to give.

Type III, The Giver, figure the Macedonian church, "The Generous Heart," verse 2 Corinthians 8:2, symbol open hands.
The reader whose hand opens easily, who gives from the heart. Risk: giving beyond capacity, neglecting their own foundation, debt from generosity.

Type IV, The Builder, figure Nehemiah, "The Systematic Restorer," verse Nehemiah 4:6, symbol a wall with a plumb line.
The reader who creates systems, finishes structures, restores order. Risk: making an idol of the plan, missing spontaneity, instrumentalizing people and process.

Each type's report content must reference its own figure, verses, strengths, and blind spots. A Guardian must never receive Solomon's content. Type-specific data lives in a single source of truth and is selected dynamically.

## 13. Report writing doctrine

The full report is the real product.

If the report feels generic, the business dies. If it feels personal, the reader keeps it, rereads it, and recommends it.

The report must produce the reaction:

> "I did not expect it to be this specific."

The report must be:

- personalized to their answers and free text
- specific, not categorical
- emotional but grounded
- structured and easy to read
- spiritually serious
- literary in quality
- long enough to feel valuable, with no filler

The report must avoid:

- generic AI text
- exaggerated promises
- dangerous financial or medical language
- prosperity-preacher tone
- absolute claims
- repetition

Report structure (10 sections, roughly 20 to 30 pages, generated by multiple focused AI calls sharing one system prompt):

1. Your Reading (the personalized hard truth, the emotional heart)
2. Your Four-Dimensional Score
3. Your Hidden Gift
4. Your Growth Roadmap (the five blind spots, made concrete)
5. Your Debt Strategy
6. Your Investment Philosophy
7. Your Giving Strategy
8. Your 30-Day Path
9. Scripture for Your Journey (30 curated verses with reflections)
10. A Final Word (closing letter and benediction)

Plus front matter (cover, verse page, contents) and back matter (disclaimer).

## 14. Delivery experience

Delivery must exceed the price paid.

The reader pays for a low-ticket report but must feel they received something more valuable than $19.

Important elements:

- clean confirmation page
- instant access
- clear delivery email
- a beautifully typeset PDF (editorial-biblical: serif type, gold line-art symbols, drop caps, white content pages, parchment only on cover and back)
- personalized titles and salutation
- no bugs, no delays

The delivery email must be short and clear: confirmation, download link, what it contains, how to read it, support contact.

PDF generation happens on purchase (Stripe webhook), not on quiz completion, so we never spend AI tokens on non-buyers.

## 15. Pay-it-forward (sponsored reports)

Talanthos includes a generosity mechanic that fits the brand: readers can fund a report for someone else.

Framing: "Bless someone else," never "donation" (we are a commercial SRL, not a registered charity).

Offered on the thank-you page after purchase, the moment the heart is most open.

Structure: four options, $19 (bless one), $57 (bless three, marked "most chosen"), $95 (bless five), and a custom amount.

Mechanism (must be real, never a false claim):
- Dual model: an anonymous pool plus the option to gift directly to a named person by email.
- Funded credits accumulate in the pool. People in genuine need can request a sponsored report through a simple, dignified form (email plus one short sentence) with anti-abuse limits.
- Transparent framing: gifts build a fund until requested.

Rules:

- never use guilt or pressure
- always make declining easy
- never overpromise the impact
- never charge anyone without explicit, clear consent

This mechanic builds AOV and embodies the brand without resembling the dark patterns of Prayers Academy.

## 16. Ad creative doctrine

The creative is the targeting.

For Talanthos, Meta and Pinterest creatives must test many emotional angles, all within the transformation framing.

Rules:

- hook in the first 1 to 2 seconds
- simple text
- clear visual
- no overdesign
- no money iconography (no dollars, coins, graphs)
- no wealth promises
- no fear or manipulation
- mobile-first
- editorial-biblical aesthetic, not Christian-Pinterest kitsch

Good angles:

- "What does your money reveal about your soul?"
- "There are four biblical money types. Most Christians never learn theirs."
- "Why do I keep repeating the same pattern with money?"
- "The Bible has 2,000 verses about money. Which one is about you?"
- "A hard truth, told with compassion."

Angles to avoid:

- guaranteed wealth or breakthrough
- aggressive fear
- prosperity gospel language
- "secret" hype
- anything that smells like a scam

## 17. Organic distribution

Talanthos grows through editorial content that fits each platform natively. Faceless by design.

Primary channel: Pinterest. Faceless-friendly, dense Christian audience, compounding traffic, low production cost (graphic pins in the editorial aesthetic).

Secondary: TikTok faceless with AI voice (ElevenLabs), 60-second narrations in the brand voice.

Later (month 2+): blog SEO on high-intent topics ("what does the Bible say about debt," "Bible verses about wealth," "biblical money personality types").

Excluded by founder constraint:

- no live events of any kind (no webinars, calls, cohorts)
- no founder filming or founder-as-figure
- future AI voice or video only when imperceptible to users

Principles:

- content must feel native, never spammy
- no fake testimonials presented as real
- no invented statistics or fake authority
- automate research, drafting, scheduling; never automate deception

## 18. Tracking and KPIs

Each funnel step must be measured separately. Tracking must be verified in the database before the first ad runs. (Lesson carried from prior projects: untracked leads contaminate every decision that follows.)

Key KPIs:

1. Ad CTR
2. CPC
3. Landing page view rate
4. Quiz start rate
5. Quiz completion rate
6. Email submit rate
7. Result view rate
8. Paywall view rate
9. Checkout start rate
10. Purchase conversion rate
11. CPA / CAC
12. AOV (including pay-it-forward)
13. Refund rate
14. Repeat / future product attach rate

Pixel plus Conversion API, with every Purchase event verified landing in the database before spend.

Success checkpoints (first 90 days):

- Day 30: 20+ paid sales to justify scaling
- Day 60: CAC < $25, AOV > $30, refund rate < 10%
- Day 90: $3,000 to $5,000 per month baseline

If below thresholds at day 90: postmortem on copy, audience, pricing, or product.

## 19. Development order

Strategic build order:

1. PDF report pipeline (design + content) for one type, validated
2. All four types (dynamic type data, no hardcoding)
3. Stripe checkout + webhook + PDF trigger
4. Email delivery + nurture sequence
5. Tracking (Pixel + Conversion API) verified in database
6. Pay-it-forward / sponsored reports add-on
7. Pre-launch checklist (fix false claims, audit content, full end-to-end test)
8. Launch (first ads + Pinterest + TikTok)

Important:

The learning funnel is one product done excellently, not many products half-built. After Phase 1 is validated, the ecosystem replicates on proven logic.

## 20. AI operational rules

Any AI working on Talanthos must follow these rules:

1. Never invent financial promises or guaranteed returns.
2. Never give licensed financial advice (no specific securities, no "buy X").
3. Never use prosperity-gospel language.
4. Never use fear or manipulation.
5. Never produce generic, categorical text where personalization is promised.
6. Never use absolute claims.
7. Never push upsells aggressively or too early.
8. Never change the brand positioning without reason.
9. Keep the voice calm, pastoral, literary, and unflinching but hopeful.
10. Quote Scripture accurately.
11. Always include the spiritual-guidance disclaimer on money-topic sections.

For copy: short, specific, emotionally true, reader-oriented, no jargon.
For reports: personalized, structured, literary, compassionate, with disclaimers where needed.
For ad creatives: clear hook, editorial aesthetic, little text, emotion-first, no overdesign, no money iconography.

## 21. Ethical boundaries

Talanthos uses Scripture, spiritual interpretation, and self-knowledge. It must respect clear limits:

- it does not replace professional financial, legal, or tax advice
- it does not exploit fear or financial desperation
- it does not manipulate vulnerable people
- it does not guarantee wealth or outcomes
- it does not invent testimonials presented as real
- it does not claim absolute spiritual authority
- it does not make false claims about its scale, impact, or where money goes

Every report carries: "This report offers spiritual guidance, not professional financial, legal, or tax advice. Seek qualified counsel for decisions pertaining to your specific situation."

Honesty is the moat. The entire brand is positioned against the dark patterns of competitors. Any dishonest claim, fake number, or hidden charge would destroy the one thing that differentiates Talanthos.

## 22. Decision rules

When a decision must be made, ask:

1. Does it increase clarity for the reader?
2. Does it increase trust?
3. Does it deepen self-recognition without manipulation?
4. Does it reduce friction?
5. Does it keep the Talanthos voice?
6. Does it help the funnel learn faster?
7. Can it be measured?
8. Can it be replicated across products?
9. Does it stay within the founder constraints (faceless, no live, honest)?

If the answer is no to most, do not implement it.

## 23. Technical doctrine

Funnels and products must be built modularly.

Stack: Next.js + TypeScript + Tailwind, Supabase (Pro), Stripe (DEXARA SRL), Resend, Vercel, Claude API for personalized readings, ElevenLabs for future AI voice. Puppeteer (HTML/CSS) for PDF rendering.

Architecture must allow:

- A/B testing of copy and price
- per-step event tracking
- product segmentation
- email delivery automation
- cross-sell rules
- future subscription rules

Type-specific data lives in a single source of truth, selected dynamically. Critical copy and rules are not hardcoded where they could be configurable.

PDF content is generated by multiple focused AI calls sharing one system prompt, on purchase, with a fast html-only test path for visual iteration without spending tokens.

Claude Code rule (always prepend): "RULES: Only touch files directly related to this bug/task. No refactoring. No other changes. Confirm before each edit."

## 24. North Star

North Star for Talanthos:

> A reader must feel they received a reading so personal and so true that they keep it, reread it, and recommend it, and feel called toward change because of it.

If the report produces that, ads and funnels can be optimized around it.

If the report does not produce that, no funnel will save the business long term.

## 25. Operational conclusion

Talanthos must be developed as a replicable system of quizzes, personalized interpretations, and reports, all within one coherent spiritual universe.

The products are not isolated. They are different entry points into the same interpretive world.

Real priorities, in order:

1. Trust
2. Personalization
3. Self-recognition
4. Delivery beyond expectation
5. Honest generosity (pay-it-forward, done truly)
6. Retention through the ecosystem

Final rule:

> We do not build pages. We build moments of self-recognition that lead naturally toward a true reading, a real decision, and a deeper walk.
